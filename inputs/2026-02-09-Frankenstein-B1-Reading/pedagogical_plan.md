# Pedagogical Plan: Frankenstein (Oxford Futures 3)

> **STRUCTURE GATE**: This plan structures the lesson into clear Phases with mandatory Bridge Slides (What/Why/Tips).

## Phase 1: Context & Genre
**Goal**: Orient students to the Gothic/Sci-Fi genre and the author.

### Slide Sequence
1.  **Title Slide**: FRANKENSTEIN (B2 Reading)
2.  **Helicopter Mission**: "Your Mission" (Analyze, Profile, Sequence, Decode)
3.  **Segue**: "Phase 1: Genre & Context" ("Get ready to analyze")
4.  **Bridge (Strategy)**:
    *   **Task**: Genre Analysis (Task 1)
    *   **What**: Decide if this is horror, sci-fi, or comedy.
    *   **Why**: Genre tells us what rules the story follows.
    *   **Tip**: Look for "dark" words or "science" words.
5.  **Task 1**: Genre Selection (Single Slide)
6.  **Answer 1**: Early Sci-Fi / Horror (Answer Detail)
7.  **Bridge (Strategy)**:
    *   **Task**: Author Profile (Task 2)
    *   **What**: Find facts about Mary Shelley.
    *   **Why**: The author's life often inspires the story.
    *   **Tip**: Scan for dates (1816) and names (Byron).
8.  **Task 2**: Scan text for Mary Shelley profile (Split Table)
9.  **Answer 2**: Profile Key (Answer Detail)

## Phase 2: Vocabulary & Prediction
**Goal**: Pre-teach keywords and engage students with the text before detailed reading.

### Slide Sequence
10. **Segue**: "Phase 2: Vocabulary" ("Get ready to guess")
11. **Bridge (Strategy)**:
    *   **Task**: Word Guess (Task 3)
    *   **What**: Choose the best word to complete the sentence.
    *   **Why**: Context helps you learn new words without a dictionary.
    *   **Tip**: Is the sentence positive or negative?
12. **Task 3 (Part 1)**: Questions 1-3 (Auto-Animate: Gap Fill / Choice)
    *   *Note*: Use "Auto-Animate" to highlight selected words.
13. **Task 3 (Part 2)**: Questions 4-5 (Auto-Animate)
14. **Answer 3**: Full sentences with definitions (Answer Detail)

## Phase 3: The Story (Reading Comprehension)
**Goal**: Reconstruct the narrative arc and understand cause-and-effect.

### Slide Sequence
15. **Segue**: "Phase 3: The Story" ("Get ready to read")
16. **Reading Slides**: Present the text chunks (Ex 4 - implied reading time).
17. **Bridge (Strategy)**:
    *   **Task**: Order the Events (Task 4)
    *   **What**: Put the story events (A-F) in the right order.
    *   **Why**: Understanding *sequence* helps you retell the story.
    *   **Tip**: Look for "First", "Then", "After".
18. **Task 4 (Part 1)**: Items A-C (Ranking/Ordering - Auto-Animate)
19. **Task 4 (Part 2)**: Items D-F (Ranking/Ordering - Auto-Animate)
20. **Answer 4**: Correct Sequence 1-6 (Answer Detail)

## Phase 4: Deep Analysis
**Goal**: Analyze character motivations and themes.

### Slide Sequence
21. **Segue**: "Phase 4: Analysis" ("Get ready to think")
22. **Bridge (Strategy)**:
    *   **Task**: Deep Analysis (Task 5)
    *   **What**: Answer questions about the narrator.
    *   **Why**: The "Voice" of the story changes how we feel.
    *   **Tip**: Is the narrator happy or sad about the past?
23. **Task 5**: Questions 1-3 (Open Questions - Single Slide)
24. **Answer 5**: Analysis Answers (Answer Detail)
25. **Bridge (Strategy)**:
    *   **Task**: Character Flaws (Task 7)
    *   **What**: Identify Victor's bad personality traits.
    *   **Why**: Flaws create conflict and tragedy.
    *   **Tip**: "Arrogant" means thinking you are better than God.
26. **Task 7**: Check the Flaws (Multiple Choice/Checklist - Auto-Animate)
27. **Answer 7**: Obsessive, Irresponsible, Reckless (Answer Detail)
28. **Bridge (Strategy)**:
    *   **Task**: Themes (Task 8)
    *   **What**: What is the main message?
    *   **Why**: Themes apply the story to real life.
    *   **Tip**: Which lesson did Victor learn the hard way?
29. **Task 8**: Select Themes (Multiple Choice - Auto-Animate)
30. **Answer 8**: Dangerous Knowledge (Answer Detail)
