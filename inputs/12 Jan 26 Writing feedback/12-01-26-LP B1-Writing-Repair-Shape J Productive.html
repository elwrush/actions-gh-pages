The Grammar Repair Shop

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to self-correct common grammatical errors while writing a descriptive paragraph tailored to their current level of grammatical proficiency.

Date: 12-01-26

Systems/Skills: Writing (Accuracy & Self-Correction)

CEFR Level: B1 (Mixed Ability: A2-B2)

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: Individual Student Feedback Reports (JSON/PDF), Grammar Repair Worksheet

Assessment: CA (Writing Task)

Lesson Stages

Stage Aim Procedure Time Interaction
STAGE ONE: The Diagnostic Report (Lead-in)
1 To frame feedback as a constructive tool for improvement The "Car Mechanic" Analogy
  • T draws a car with smoke coming out. "This car runs, but it has problems."
  • "When you go to a mechanic, they give you a Report. Broken brake light. Low oil."
  • "Today, YOU are the mechanics. Your previous writing has been analyzed."
  • Hand out the Student Feedback Reports (simulated/real from previous task).
5 T-Ss
STAGE TWO: Analyzing Repair Targets
2 To identify individual high-frequency errors Mission Prep: Selecting Targets
  • Direct Ss to Page 1 of the Grammar Repair Worksheet.
  • "Look at your Feedback Report. What mistakes did the computer find most?" (e.g., Missing Articles, Wrong Verb Form).
  • Ss write down their "Top 3 Repair Targets" on the worksheet (e.g., 1. Past Tense, 2. Plurals, 3. Commas).
  • T monitors and helps interpret technical terms (e.g., "Determiner" = a/an/the).
 8 Indiv
STAGE THREE: Dot-Dash Planning (Content & Structure)
3 To plan content using level-appropriate structures Differentiated Task Setup
  • Ss turn to their specific level page (A2, B1, or B2).
  • A2 (Recipe): Plan 3 Steps. First -> Then -> Finally.
  • B1 (Trap): Plan using S-C-R (Situation: Good dish -> Complication: The mistake to avoid -> Resolution: Success).
  • B2 (Signature): Plan 2 paragraphs focusing on 'Complexity' vs 'Reward'.
  • Ss use the margin or scratch paper to "Dot-Dash" their main ideas before writing sentences.
8 T-Ss -> Indiv
STAGE FOUR: The Repair Shop (Drafting)
4 To produce text while actively monitoring for specific errors Drafting with Radar Checks
  • Ss write their paragraphs in the "Writing Area" of the worksheet.
  • Critical Constraint: As they write, they must check their "Self-Correction Radar" box (bottom of their worksheet page).
  • T circulates: "Check your radar. Did you use an article there? Is that plural?"
  • Encourage "Pause and Repair" - stop after every sentence to check against the Targets from Stage 2.
 15 Indiv
STAGE FIVE: Quality Control (Peer Review)
5 To practice spotting errors in other contexts The Final Inspection
  • Pairs swap worksheets (try to match levels if possible).
  • "You are now the Quality Inspector. Check their Radar points."
  • If B1 student checks B1 paper: "Did they warn about the Trap?"
  • Circle any mistakes politely using pencil.
  • Return to owner for final polish.
 5 Ss-Ss
STAGE SIX: Checkout (Reflection)
6 To consolidate the self-correction habit Exit Ticket
  • Ask: "Which 'Repair Target' was the hardest to fix today?"
  • "Next time you write, imagine the Radar is still there."
  • Collect worksheets for assessment.
 5 T-Ss

Total Time: 46 minutes

Answer Key

Answers will vary as this is a productive writing task. Refer to the "Self-Correction Radar" on each worksheet level for specific assessment criteria.
