Presentation Skills 1: Structure

Objective: By the end of the lesson, learners will be better able to structure a book or story presentation using a hook, a four-part story arc (exposition), a personal analysis, and a final recommendation.

Date: 29-12-25

Systems/Skills: Speaking / Productive Skills

CEFR Level: B1-B2

Teacher: Ed Rush

Duration: 46 minutes

Materials: presentation-structure-planning-sheet.html, students' own books/stories from Reading Lab.

Assessment: Lesson participation and worksheet completion.

Lesson Stages

Stage Aim Procedure Time Interaction
STAGE ONE: Build Interest (The "Fear" Hook)
1 To activate schemata regarding public speaking anxiety and introduce strategies. Fears & Fixes
  • Discussion: Show slide: "Why are people so afraid of public speaking, and how can we fix it?" Elicit ideas.
  • Video: Watch Public Speaking Tips (YouTube Short).
  • Reaction: Ask class to explain their own fears and suggest their own "fixes" based on the video (e.g., preparation, structure). Transition to today's fix: Structure.
6 T-Ss
STAGE TWO: Preparation for Task (Introduction to Structure)
2 To clarify the components of a successful presentation structure. Mapping the Presentation
  • Hand out the **Presentation Planning Sheet**.
  • Briefly go through the 4 stages: Hook, Exposition (Story Arc), Analysis, Recommendation.
  • Focus on **The Hook**: Demo a "Question" vs. "Imagine" hook using a popular story (e.g., Harry Potter).
  • Focus on **The Story Arc**: Briefly explain Rising Action and Climax.
 8 T-Ss
3 To allow Ss to brainstorm and plan their specific story. Brainstorming
  • Ss select a book from Reading Lab or a story they love.
  • Ss work individually to fill in the **Hook** and **Exposition** sections.
  • Teacher monitors and assists with vocabulary for characters/setting.
 8 S
STAGE THREE: Task Stage (Personalizing and Refining)
4 To push Ss into deeper analysis and recommendation. Deep Dive & Recommendation
  • Explain the **Analysis** section: Encourage Ss to find one thing they *disliked* to make it a more balanced talk.
  • Explain **The Recommendation**: Narrow it down to "Would you recommend this and why?" (referencing the new worksheet section).
  • Ss complete the rest of the worksheet.
 8 S
5 To provide a low-stakes verbal practice of the structure. Peer Pitch (30-second version)
  • In pairs, Ss give a "mini" version of their talk focusing only on the **Hook** and the **Climax**.
  • Partner gives feedback: "Was the hook interesting?" "Was the climax clear?"
 8 Ss-Ss
STAGE FOUR: Feedback and Wrap-up
6 To summarize learnings and perform a self-check. Self-Check & Future Planning
  • Ss use the checklist at the bottom of the worksheet to evaluate their plan.
  • Tell Ss to keep this sheet for Lesson 2, where we will add **Useful Language** and signposting.
  • Final feedback session: One or two Ss share their Hook with the whole class.
 4 T-Ss

Total Time: 46 minutes

Answer Key / Teacher Notes

No fixed answer key as students use their own stories. Review planning sheets for:

  • Structure (Presence of all 4 stages)
  • Engagement (Does the hook actually hook?)
  • Clarity (Is the 'Rising Action' distinguished from the 'Climax'?)
